Smart Coaster Model Preview

This page is a browser-friendly structural preview derived from the current OpenSCAD model. It is meant to help you inspect OLED placement, light-band routing, light window position, load-cell structure and shell assembly logic without needing a CAD viewer.

Assembled View

Front preview of the current concept. This focuses on the OLED position and the mid-body light band.

Overall shell: 100 mm diameter and 35 mm tall.
Two main shells plus a separate floating cup_insert.
Load path goes through the center insert into the bar load cell.
OLED and Type-C positions are generic for now and can be refined to match real hardware.
In the current generic layout, the front OLED opening interrupts the front section of the light window. So the light band still wraps around the body, but the forward segment is not continuous.

Light-Band Wrap

The LED strip sits inside the mid-body ring chamber and wraps around the shell circumference. The front OLED window cuts a small gap in the forward light section.

Top Cup Area

The cup does not press directly on the outer shell. It sits on the floating insert, which then transfers force into the center of the load cell.

Assembly Notes

The current model is enough to review structure. For cleaner mounting with less glue, the next pass should be based on the real module dimensions.

OLED: thin double-sided tape or a tiny amount of hot glue for the first prototype.
Light strip: use its own adhesive first, then reinforce small spots with neutral silicone.
Load cell: use screws rather than glue.
ESP32 and Type-C board: can become semi tool-less once the real measurements are known.
Matching OpenSCAD source: hydrapet_smart_coaster.scad. If needed, this preview can be upgraded into a parameter-linked page where the HTML labels match the model variables one by one.
